Banana Quest

  • Originality: I've never played a point-and-click adventure game, so I can't say for sure. I would like to think that my story/setting is original for an adventure game.
  • Technical Merit: It plays with only the mouse, no keyboard required.
  • Prototype Postmortem: I learned how to move a sprite using mouse and have a map larger than window size. I experimented a lot in attempt to have the player click once and have the character move to the location of the mouse, but it always bugged out.
  • Prototype Assets:
    • Monkey (recolored): Phaser dude
    • Music: Donkey Kong Returns OST
    • Sound effect: Mario Coin
  • Prototype Closest Other Game: Similar to the phaser tutorial, but is more like a top-down view than a platformer.
  • High Concept: A short game that is meant to be easy and relaxing to play. Play as a monkey on a quest to find all of the bananas scattered around the map.
  • Theme: The theme is a point-and-click adventure game.
  • Mandated Variety: This is an adventure point-and-click game that varies each time in the location of the bananas. All banana locations are randomly generated.
  • Prototype Goal: this game focuses on movement with mouse controls with moving camera
  • Player Experience Goals: A relaxing gaming experience, with nice music and sounds.
  • Gameplay: As this is a point-and-click adventure game, the controls are simply to click at a spot and the sprite will head in that direction. For optimal movement, hold the mouse button and to have the sprite follow.
  • Strategies: Not really any strategy to the game as bananas are randomly generated.
  • Story/Setting/Premise: You are a monkey that has not eaten in 2 days. Luckily, there are plenty of bananas scattered throughout the forest. Go on a quest to find the bananas!
  • Target Audience: casual, for-fun players.
  • Play Time: Less than a minute
